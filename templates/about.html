AstralCTF

{{ Header }}

AstralCTF was conceived and developed by three high-school students (Maxime Dowla, Nicolas Macchioni, and Katie Seidl) who were inspired by other Capture The Flags (CTFs) they had partaken in. They felt like their creation would fulfill an important need and they wanted to spread cyber awareness and other aspects of cyber security for other high schoolers and others who may not have had prior access to these tools and information. The reason for the name “AstralCTF” was because they felt as though a “cloud” is not an accurate representation of the IoT, and the name should more represent that of a constellation since everything is connected more in that sense. Because they wanted to address all types of constellations or the vast majority of them, Astral felt more appropriate. The story is an original that they made because they felt like there needed to be some motivation to solve the problems, and so it has elements of both mystery and humor.

Note: Anyone who is able to complete all advanced problems will have a chance to join the developing team as a way for further potential in areas such as creativity. This is not a guarantee.

Categories:

The categories will unlock as you solve more problems, and these categories consist of Steg, Base, Ciphers, XOR, AES/SHA/MD5, Packet Tracer/networking, SQL injections, and more. These subjects pop up frequently in cyber and so these are practical problems and help train for future problems. They encourage the use of creative thinking as well as expect the student to do some of their own problem solving and self-learning. Basic information and hints are scattered throughout the problems to aid the learning of these topics. The categories and problems open up with the story as it develops, and the flow of the CTF will feel smooth. We attempt to make the flow vary, although if you connect the dots of the problems you probably won’t get a nice looking constellation as smol effort was made there. The reason for the “flow” is to make sure that the user is learning a breadth of techniques used in cyber security.

Scoring System:

Harder problems are worth more, and the next problem in that category doesn’t unlock until all problems of that tier have been solved. The amount of points a problem is worth is equal to its difficulty or time spent on it. Undecided whether it’s first to finish, most problems solved, or if this will simply be a personal thing people can do on their own time.

ToS: In order to ensure that this competition can continue to be hosted, we ask only that you do not perform any unrelated malicious activity that you may learn in this competition. Several of the methods used here may be illegal and could cause serious legal issues.
